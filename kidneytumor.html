Overview

What ResSGA-Net changes in the diagnostic workflow.

Early kidney tumor detection can radically improve treatment outcomes, but scan interpretation remains time-intensive and vulnerable to subtle misses under pressure. This landing page frames the model as a precision support layer for clinicians, not a replacement.

The system starts with normalized kidney CT imagery, then extracts stable deep features through ResNet50 while gated self-attention amplifies the regions most likely to contain diagnostically meaningful structures.

A Swin Transformer adds long-range spatial awareness, allowing the model to interpret both fine tumor boundaries and global tissue context before producing a final classification across normal, benign, and malignant outcomes.

Method

A five-stage architecture shaped for controlled clinical intelligence.

The system is designed as a single information pipeline where each stage contributes a distinct geometric function: clean inputs, stable features, focused regions, wider contextual understanding, and final diagnostic classification.

Stage 01

Preprocessing

Resize to 224×224, normalize intensity ranges, and augment scan data for balanced training behavior.

Stage 02

ResNet50

Residual learning stabilizes feature extraction across depth and preserves clinically useful low- and high-level patterns.

Stage 03

Gated Attention

Attention gates suppress background noise and intensify the feature regions most relevant to tumor localization.

Stage 04

Swin Transformer

Shifted-window attention captures broader spatial context for shape, margin, and structural interpretation.

Stage 05

Classification

A fused representation produces normal, benign, or malignant predictions through a final softmax decision layer.

Results

Strong benchmark performance with resilience under distortion.

Evaluated on 12,000 balanced CT images across three outcome classes, ResSGA-Net outperformed common baseline networks under the same training conditions while remaining stable under noisy and low-contrast inputs.
